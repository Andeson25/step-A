Step A testing service
- a new initiative

Welcome to this learning module on the new Strep A testing service available to customers. The service allows a trained pharmacist to assess a customer presenting with sore throat to determine if they are suffering from a streptococcal throat infection. Following a swab test, if the customer shows a positive result for the presence of Strep A, the trained pharmacist can supply antibiotics for the treatment of the infection, if appropriate, via an in-store Patient Group Direction (PGD).

Content developed by CIG Healthcare Partnership.

This module will:

  • Highlight the vital role of the Healthcare Partner in engaging customers with the service and providing OTC advice
  • Explain how to determine a customer’s eligibility for the service
  • Describe how to undertake the Strep A swab test
  • Explain what to do in the event of a positive, negative or invalid test result
  • Detail important advice to give to customers if an antibiotic is supplied via the PGD

Sore throat and Strep A


Sore throat is a common condition which can be due to a viral or bacterial cause. Both are normally a self-limiting and generally resolve within two weeks.* An example of a bacterial cause of sore throat is streptococcal infection.


Streptococcal bacteria are highly contagious. They can spread through airborne droplets when someone with the infection coughs or sneezes, or through droplets on hard surfaces such as door handles, toys or cups.


Group A streptococcus infections (known as Strep A) are characterised by pain at the back of the throat which feels worse when swallowing, along with redness in the throat and possibly puss on the tonsils along with a fever of over 38.5°C. Children may also have a rash. Although Strep A is not considered dangerous, it can lead to complications including scarlet fever, rheumatic fever and inflammation of the kidneys.


Always keep the above in mind when speaking to customers about their symptoms so you can provide the appropriate advice.

* Georgalas et al, 2014; Kenealy, 2014; BMJ Best Practice, 2016a

Approaching customers
Role of the Healthcare Partner

Although a pharmacist needs to assess the customer to determine whether they have a Strep A infection, the Healthcare Partner’s role is vital in speaking to customers about the service. Customers may not come into the pharmacy actively seeking advice on the service; they are more likely to come in asking for advice on their symptoms. Below are ways in which you could build a good rapport with customers and promote the service:

Step 1
Go through the WWHAM framework

Use the framework to help the customer to explore their symptoms fully. 2 3

Step 2
If sore throat is prevalent

Inform the customer that an in-store service is available which can test for a bacterial infection (note: the service is only available to customers who are 18 years or over).

Step 3
If the customer does not want to undertake the Strep A testing service

You could advise on OTC products if appropriate to help with their symptoms and tell them to come back if symptoms do not get better.

Step 4
If they want to explore the service further

Take them into the consultation room and inform them that the pharmacist will be in soon to explain more about the service.

Steps to delivering a great consultation

If you have a customer who wants to determine whether or not they have a bacterial infection, it is important for you to provide detailed information on the service before undertaking the consultation. This will help put them at ease and understand what to expect from the test.


Before undertaking the swab test, you will need to ensure that they are eligible for the service. There are several instances where supply of antibiotics under the PGD will not be suitable. You can download the exclusion criteria here for further information. Familiarise yourself with this and check the criteria with customers before undertaking the service.

To be able to deliver a successful consultation, make sure you:

  • Are fully familiar with the PGD
  • Have all your equipment ready
  • Go through the FeverPain criteria
  • Carefully undertake the swab test

Inclusion criteria
To be eligible for the service, customers must:

1

Be 18 years over

2

Have a FeverPAIN score of 4 or 5 (see details on following screen)

3

Test positive on throat swab for streptococcal infection

Exclusion criteria
If a customer meets the exclusion criteria:

1

Advise the customer to see their GP today. Alternatively they can call 111, attend an NHS walk-in centre or urgent care centre

2

If a severe infection is suspected (e.g. a quinsy or epiglottis) or there is any respiratory compromise, direct the customer to the nearest A&E department urgently
